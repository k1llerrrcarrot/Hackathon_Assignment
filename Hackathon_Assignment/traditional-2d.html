Traditional 2D Fighting Games

The games that started it all! The goal is simple: Reduce your opponent's health to 0 by hitting them with a wide range of moves! This basic gameplay loop would be adopted by most of the sub-genres, with exception of the platform fighters.

Traditional 2D Fighting Games are often characterized by relatively complicated inputs to perform special moves. This is also a tradition that is still present nowadays in this and other sub-genres. Various games have experimented with simpler input styles and over the years, while still here, inputs have become more lenient and easier to perform. The ability to execute a string of moves is seen as an important part of skill expression in the genre, which is why the complex inputs are still around.

Some examples of 2D Fighting Games:

  • The Street Fighter series
  • The King of Fighters series
  • The Mortal Kombat series
  • The Killer Instinct series
  • FIGHTING EX LAYER
